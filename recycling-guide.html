Plastic Recycling Guide

Learn how to properly recycle different types of plastics and understand the recycling codes.

What to Recycle

  • Plastic bottles (water, soda, juice)
  • Plastic containers (yogurt, margarine)
  • Plastic bags and wraps
  • Plastic food containers
  • Plastic cleaning product bottles
  • Plastic milk jugs

What NOT to Recycle

  • Plastic utensils
  • Plastic straws
  • Plastic toys
  • Plastic hangers
  • Plastic bags with food residue
  • Styrofoam containers

Preparation Steps

  1. Rinse containers thoroughly
  2. Remove caps and labels
  3. Flatten containers to save space
  4. Check for recycling symbols
  5. Separate by plastic type if required

Recycling Codes

1 PET - Polyethylene Terephthalate
2 HDPE - High-Density Polyethylene
3 PVC - Polyvinyl Chloride
4 LDPE - Low-Density Polyethylene
5 PP - Polypropylene
6 PS - Polystyrene

Paper Recycling Guide

Discover how to properly recycle paper products and maximize their environmental benefits.

What to Recycle

  • Newspapers and magazines
  • Office paper and envelopes
  • Cardboard boxes
  • Paper bags
  • Junk mail and catalogs
  • Paperboard (cereal boxes)

What NOT to Recycle

  • Waxed paper
  • Paper towels and tissues
  • Paper with food residue
  • Sticky notes
  • Shredded paper (check local rules)
  • Paper with plastic coatings

Preparation Steps

  1. Remove any plastic or metal attachments
  2. Flatten cardboard boxes
  3. Remove food residue
  4. Separate by paper type
  5. Keep paper dry and clean

Metal Recycling Guide

Learn how to recycle aluminum, steel, and other metals effectively.

What to Recycle

  • Aluminum cans (soda, beer)
  • Steel cans (food, soup)
  • Aluminum foil and trays
  • Metal lids and caps
  • Empty aerosol cans
  • Scrap metal

What NOT to Recycle

  • Paint cans with paint
  • Propane tanks
  • Electronics with metal
  • Metal with sharp edges
  • Contaminated metal
  • Metal furniture

Preparation Steps

  1. Rinse cans thoroughly
  2. Remove labels if possible
  3. Crush cans to save space
  4. Separate aluminum from steel
  5. Check for hazardous materials

Electronics Recycling Guide

Learn how to properly dispose of electronic waste and find recycling centers.

What to Recycle

  • Computers and laptops
  • Mobile phones and tablets
  • Televisions and monitors
  • Printers and scanners
  • Batteries (all types)
  • Chargers and cables

Why Recycle Electronics

  • Prevent toxic materials from landfills
  • Recover valuable metals
  • Reduce environmental pollution
  • Conserve natural resources
  • Create jobs in recycling industry

Preparation Steps

  1. Backup and erase all data
  2. Remove batteries if possible
  3. Remove personal information
  4. Check for recycling programs
  5. Find certified e-waste recyclers

Organic Waste Composting Guide

Learn how to compost organic waste and create nutrient-rich soil for your garden.

What to Compost

  • Fruit and vegetable scraps
  • Coffee grounds and filters
  • Tea bags
  • Eggshells
  • Grass clippings
  • Leaves and yard waste

What NOT to Compost

  • Meat and fish scraps
  • Dairy products
  • Oils and fats
  • Pet waste
  • Diseased plants
  • Weeds with seeds

Composting Steps

  1. Choose a composting method
  2. Layer green and brown materials
  3. Keep compost moist but not wet
  4. Turn compost regularly
  5. Monitor temperature and progress

Regular Collection
